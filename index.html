New Orleans · Must-Do Classics
Before you leave NOLA.
Seafood · Est. 1970
Drago's Seafood.
They invented the charbroiled oyster. Since 1970, Drago's has been the kind of place NOLA locals bring out-of-towners to prove a point. Order the oysters. Order the mac and cheese. You'll understand.
✦ NOLA Classic
Sno-Balls · Est. 1934
Hansen's Sno-Bliz.
Three generations, one machine, one block on Tchoupitoulas. Ernest Hansen invented the sno-ball machine in 1934 and the family has been making them ever since. The line is always there. The line is always worth it.
✦ NOLA Classic
Cafe · Since 1862
Cafe Du Monde.
Open 24 hours, since 1862, right on the French Quarter. Beignets dusted in powdered sugar, cafe au lait in a branded mug, and a kitchen you can watch through the glass. This is the NOLA morning ritual.
✦ NOLA Classic
NOLA Area Hidden Gem
Southern's Metairie.
📍 Metairie, LA · Just outside New Orleans

The tourists are eating beignets on Bourbon Street. You are at Southern's. No frills, no hype, just a red entrance mat, a line out the door, and the kind of fried chicken sandwich that makes you question every meal you have had before it. This is what locals actually eat.

Indiehouse Fragrances.

Scent Boutique · ATL
Where finding your signature scent is the whole experience.

Indiehouse carries over 100 indie and niche fragrance brands you won't find at any mall counter. But the real reason to go? Book a consultation, sit in one of the reserved leather chairs, and let them build something made for you. Oh, and the gold skeleton keeps watch over the whole thing.

Featured Spot · Black-Owned ✊🏾

Black Coffee ATL.

Visit Website →
Black Coffee ATL
Tucked inside a converted 1947 Baptist church in West Atlanta, Black Coffee ATL is one of those places that earns its reputation the right way — through the people, the craft, and the story the walls tell. Order the "Light Skin Keisha" (vanilla, honey, lavender) or the "ATL All In" sandwich and stay a while. This is what Black-owned and community-rooted looks like.
